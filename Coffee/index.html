Should We Drink Coffee Every Day?

Boosts cognitive function and alertness

Moderate coffee consumption can enhance cognitive function and alertness (Smith, 2002).

Rich in antioxidants

Coffee is rich in antioxidants, which may lower the risk of chronic diseases such as Parkinson’s and Alzheimer’s (Ősz et al., 2022).

Reduces risk of type 2 diabetes

Regular coffee intake has been linked to a decreased risk of type 2 diabetes (Salazar-Martinez et al., 2004).

Enhances physical performance and mood

Coffee can improve physical performance and mood (Goldstein et al., 2010).

Promotes overall wellness

Overall, daily coffee consumption may contribute positively to long-term wellness.

May cause anxiety and insomnia

Excess caffeine can lead to anxiety, insomnia, and elevated heart rate (Distelberg et al., 2017).

Can increase stress and reduce sleep

Long-term dependence may increase stress levels and reduce sleep quality (Makki et al., 2023).

May interact with medications

Coffee can negatively interact with certain medications and hinder nutrient absorption (Reddy et al., 2024).

Linked to high blood pressure

Heavy consumption is associated with higher blood pressure in sensitive individuals (Zhang et al., 2011).

Tolerance and withdrawal risks

Developing caffeine tolerance may reduce its benefits and lead to withdrawal symptoms (Dews, O'Brien and Bergman, 2002).
